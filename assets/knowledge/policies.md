# Corporate Procurement Policy

**Document ID:** POL-PROC-2024-01
**Effective Date:** 1 January 2025
**Last Revised:** 15 March 2025
**Approved By:** Chief Financial Officer
**Applies To:** All departments and personnel authorized to submit purchase orders

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the rules and procedures for submitting, reviewing, and approving purchase orders within the organization. All procurement activity must comply with these guidelines to ensure financial control, vendor accountability, and operational consistency.

---

## 2. Department Spending Limits

Each department is assigned a maximum per-order spending limit. Any single purchase order that exceeds the department's limit requires escalation and executive approval before it can be processed.

| Department  | Per-Order Limit (EUR) |
|-------------|----------------------:|
| Engineering |              5,000.00 |
| Marketing   |              5,000.00 |
| Operations  |             10,000.00 |
| Finance     |             15,000.00 |
| HR          |              3,000.00 |

Purchase orders that exceed the department limit will be flagged and may not be approved without written justification from the department head and sign-off from the Finance department.

---

## 3. Payment Terms

The maximum allowable payment term for any purchase order is **Net 30** (payment due within 30 calendar days of invoice date). Purchase orders specifying payment terms longer than Net 30 (e.g., Net 45, Net 60, Net 90) are not permitted under standard policy and will be flagged as a terms violation.

Acceptable payment terms:
- **Net 15** — payment due within 15 days
- **Net 30** — payment due within 30 days (maximum allowed)
- **Due on Receipt** — payment due immediately upon receipt of invoice

Any request for extended payment terms beyond Net 30 must be pre-approved in writing by the Finance department before the purchase order is submitted.

---

## 4. Approval Thresholds

Purchase orders are subject to the following approval requirements based on the total order value:

| Total Order Value (EUR) | Approval Required                          |
|-------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| Up to 500.00            | Automatic approval (no human review needed)|
| 500.01 — 2,500.00       | Department manager approval                |
| 2,500.01 — 10,000.00    | Department head + Finance review           |
| Over 10,000.00          | Executive approval (CFO or delegate)       |

These thresholds apply regardless of department spending limits. A purchase order may be within the department limit but still require managerial approval based on its total value.

---

## 5. Required Fields for Purchase Order Submission

Every purchase order submitted for processing must include the following fields. Missing required fields will cause the order to be flagged or rejected.

**Mandatory fields (absence causes hard rejection):**
- PO number (unique identifier)
- Vendor name
- At least one line item with description and quantity
- Total amount
- Currency
- Delivery date

**Strongly recommended fields (absence causes soft flag):**
- Vendor contact email
- SKU or part number for each line item
- Unit price for each line item
- Requester name and department
- Payment terms

Purchase orders missing mandatory fields cannot be auto-approved and will require human review regardless of other validation results.

---

## 6. Allowed Currencies

All purchase orders must be denominated in one of the following approved currencies:

- **EUR** — Euro (preferred)
- **BGN** — Bulgarian Lev
- **USD** — United States Dollar

Purchase orders submitted in any other currency will be flagged for review. The EUR is the primary operating currency and is preferred for all domestic and EU transactions.

---

## 7. Minimum Order Value

The minimum total value for a purchase order is **EUR 50.00**. Orders below this threshold should be consolidated with other procurement needs or handled through the petty cash process. Purchase orders with a total below the minimum will be flagged.

---

## 8. Vendor Requirements

All vendors referenced in purchase orders must meet the following criteria:

- The vendor must be registered in the approved vendor registry.
- The vendor's contract status must be **active** (not expired or suspended).
- Vendor names on purchase orders must match the registered name exactly. Minor variations (abbreviations, missing legal suffixes) will be flagged for verification but may be accepted after review.
- Orders from vendors not found in the registry will be flagged as unknown and cannot be auto-approved.

New vendor onboarding requests must be submitted to the Procurement department at least 10 business days before the intended purchase order date.

---

## 9. Pricing Compliance

Unit prices on purchase orders are compared against the current product catalog. Price deviations are assessed as follows:

- **Within 10% of catalog price:** Accepted without flag.
- **Between 10% and 25% above catalog price:** Flagged as a price mismatch for review. May indicate a negotiation issue, bulk pricing discrepancy, or catalog update needed.
- **More than 25% above catalog price:** Flagged as a significant price deviation requiring justification.

Price deviations below catalog price (i.e., the PO lists a lower price than the catalog) are accepted without flag, as they may reflect negotiated discounts.

---

## 10. Duplicate Order Prevention

The system checks for potential duplicate purchase orders based on matching vendor name, total amount, and submission date within a 7-day window. Suspected duplicates are flagged for human review before processing.

---

## 11. Policy Exceptions

Any exception to this policy must be documented in writing and approved by both the department head and the Finance department. Policy exceptions are tracked and subject to quarterly audit review.
